Romit Maulik

Margaret Butler Postdoctoral Fellow, ALCF, Argonne National Laboratory

I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Advanced Leadership Computing Facility (ALCF) at Argonne National Laboratory where I work with Dr. Prasanna Balaprakash and Dr. Bethany Lusch on machine learning development for scientific applications such as computational physics and organic chemistry.

I have a PhD from the School of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Oklahoma State University and my research deals with the use of data-driven techniques for the model order reduction of computationally expensive problems. Most of my work has revolved around the use of artificial neural networks formulated for corrections to truncated systems being evolved by transformed governing laws.

My graduate adviser was Dr. Omer San, PI at the Computational Fluid Dynamics Laboratory where we endeavored to investigate elegant combinations of machine learning algorithms and physics based models for the computational sciences.

You may find more information about my background and research interests, along with a list of publications here.
